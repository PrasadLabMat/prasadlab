Biomedical Engineering · Mechanical and Materials Engineering · Florida International University

Prasad Lab for Materials Research

We develop biobased and bioinspired materials, study the nanomechanics of biological and engineered composites, and build biomedical devices for healthcare and precision agriculture — with an emerging focus on sustainable electronic packaging and chip-level thermal management. Our work combines nanoindentation, molecular dynamics, finite element modeling, and 3D manufacturing.

Research/Funding

New

Electronic Packaging & Thermal Management

Cellulose-based flexible substrates as a sustainable alternative for electronic packaging, and bioinspired thermal composites drawing on plant vascular architecture for chip-level cooling — developed in partnership with industry toward manufacturing-ready solutions.

Data-Driven & Computational Materials Design

Machine learning and finite element frameworks for discovering and characterizing next-generation composites, nanocomposites, and hydrogels.

Funding: NSF DMREF

Plant Mechanics, Design, and Manufacturing

Molecular dynamics and experimental studies of cellulose-pectin composites and the plant cell wall's structure, mechanics, and patterning — informing bioinspired composite design and manufacturing processes such as electrospinning and 3D printing.

Funding: NSF CAREER

Regenerative Scaffolds

3D-printed bone and cardiac scaffolds, bioreactors, and diagnostic platforms supporting tissue regeneration and repair.

Funding: Florida Heart Research Foundation, NSF I-Corps, FL Dept. of Health

Biomedical Devices & Precision Agriculture

Affordable lab hardware and biosensors spanning cardiovascular diagnostics, live-cell imaging, and real-time plant health monitoring and crop protection.
